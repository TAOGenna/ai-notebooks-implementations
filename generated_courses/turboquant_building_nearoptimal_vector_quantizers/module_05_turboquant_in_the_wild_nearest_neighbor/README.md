## Key Concepts in This Module

### Recall@1@k
A metric for approximate nearest neighbour search. "Recall@1@k = 0.86" means:
for 86% of queries, the true nearest neighbour appears somewhere in the top-k
results returned by the approximate method. For retrieval applications, k=10
is a realistic setting (you re-rank the top-10 candidates with a slower exact scorer).

### The Data-Oblivious Advantage
TurboQuant never sees the training data during *indexing*. Its codebook is derived
from the Beta distribution (a mathematical property of random rotations), not from
k-means on the actual vectors. Why does this outperform data-adaptive PQ?

The random rotation *equalises* coordinate variances — every coordinate has the same
distribution after rotation. The Lloyd-Max codebook is then optimal for that known
distribution. PQ's per-coordinate k-means adapts to the empirical distribution, but
that distribution is often non-stationary, multi-modal, or poorly represented by
the training set. The rotation does better by construction.

### Why 3 Bits Is the Sweet Spot
The KL divergence between quantized and exact attention scales as O(1/4^b) (from the
distortion bound D_prod). At b=3: KL ≈ 0.001, which corresponds to an attention
distribution shift that is below human-perceptible threshold on downstream benchmarks.
At b=2: KL ≈ 0.01, which is noticeable for "needle-in-a-haystack" tasks that require
finding one critical token among thousands. This asymmetry is why 3 bits is
the paper's recommended setting.

## Analytical Questions

*Answer at Level 3 (analysis) or Level 4 (synthesis). Do not look up answers — reason
from first principles using what you have observed in the exercises.*

**Q1 — When Does PQ Win?**

TurboQuant's indexing time is near-zero because it's data-oblivious — no k-means
training needed. But PQ's codebooks are adapted to the data distribution. Under what
*distributional conditions* would PQ's data-dependent codebooks outperform TurboQuant's
universal codebook? (Hint: think about what happens when the data lies on a low-dimensional
submanifold with very non-uniform marginal distributions even after normalisation.
At what dataset size $N$ does TurboQuant's indexing speedup amortise the recall gap,
assuming you rebuild the index once per hour?)

**Q2 — Softmax Amplification of Quantization Error**

In the attention simulation, you measured KL divergence between exact and quantized
attention weights. The softmax function amplifies differences in the **largest logits**
(the "winner" tokens) and suppresses differences in the small logits (background tokens).
At 2-bit quantization, is the error more harmful for:
(a) tokens with **high** attention weight ("important" tokens), or
(b) tokens with **low** attention weight (background)?

*Connect your answer to the needle-in-a-haystack benchmark, where the model must find
one critical token among thousands. The paper achieves perfect needle-in-a-haystack at
4× compression (b=4) but shows degradation below 3 bits. Does your KL analysis support
this threshold?*

**Q3 — Rotation Matrix Overhead at Scale**

Your TurboQuantProd uses a dense random rotation matrix of shape (d, d), stored as
float32. For a production 70B parameter LLM with 80 Transformer layers, 64 attention
heads, and d=128 per head:

1. How much additional GPU memory do all rotation matrices require?
   *(Formula: n_layers × n_heads × d² × 4 bytes)*

2. At context length 32K tokens, what is the total KV cache size in float16?
   *(Formula: n_layers × n_heads × 2 × seq_len × d × 2 bytes — factor 2 for K and V)*

3. Compare (1) and (2): Is the rotation matrix overhead negligible relative to the
   KV cache savings from 3-bit quantization?

Show your arithmetic. The answer should tell you whether TurboQuant's design choice
(dense random rotation, no data-dependent preprocessing) is practical at LLM scale.

**Q4 — Designing a Better Residual Stage**

TurboQuant_prod uses 1-bit QJL on the residual. The QJL variance bound is:
```
Var ≤ (π / 2d) · ||y||² · ||r||²
```
The key term is ||r||² — the residual norm after MSE quantization.

At 3 bits (b=3), the MSE stage uses 2 bits: D_mse ≈ 0.117 (from Module 1's table).
Could you do better by replacing the 1-bit QJL residual stage with a 2-bit MSE
stage (using TurboQuantMSE at 2 bits on the residual of the 2-bit MSE stage)?
Design this "double-MSE" quantizer and compare its theoretical distortion bound
to TurboQuant_prod's D_prod at the same 3-bit total budget.
What property of QJL does this double-MSE approach lose?
